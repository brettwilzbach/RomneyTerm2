# 2. FUND TEARDOWN (12 ATOMS)

Source document: Bain Capital Q3-25 Structured Credit Strategy Profile (7-page PDF, dated Sept 30, 2025)

## CRITICAL CORRECTION

SCF is a multi-sector structured credit fund (CLO debt, CLO equity, ABS, RMBS, CMBS, esoteric securitized assets), NOT a CLO-only product. The one-pager brands it as a CLO fund, but the portfolio allocation shows ABS exposure and the team composition maps to the broader mandate: Fox (CMBS), Madden (RMBS), Garver (CLOs), Haswell (digital infrastructure). Reframing from "CLO fund" to "multi-sector structured credit" expands the addressable allocator universe dramatically.

## Atom 1: Performance Story

- $4.0B AUM (headline), inception May 2013
- 9.7% gross / 8.6% net ITD annualized
- Beat LSTA Leveraged Loan Index in 10 of 13 calendar years
- Beat BAML High Yield Index in 9 of 13 calendar years
- Cumulative: $1 invested at inception grew to $2.85 gross / $2.55 net vs $1.80 LSTA, $1.85 BAML HY
- 3-year annualized net return: 19.8% (most powerful stat in the entire document, buried in a table)
- 3-year standard deviation approximately 9.5%
- Estimated Sharpe ratio above 0.8 net (not calculated on the one-pager)

## Atom 2: Drawdown Profile

Two meaningful drawdowns in 12 years:
- -7.6% net in 2015
- -8.6% net in 2022
- Both recovered within 12 months
- Shallower than S&P 500 average annual drawdown
- This story is completely absent from the one-pager. The drawdown profile is one of SCF's strongest selling points and it gets zero commentary.

## Atom 3: Yield Decomposition

Q3 2025 portfolio yield:
- CLO Debt: 10.7%
- CLO Equity: 19.6%
- Blended (ex-cash): 12.4%
- Equity yield at cyclical highs due to elevated base rates + wide loan spreads
- Debt yield sourced from BB/B CLO tranches at average price 95.8 cents (discount to par = price upside potential)

## Atom 4: Portfolio Construction

- 236 positions across 83 CLO managers
- Top 10 concentration: 13.3%
- Non-US exposure: 17.3% (European CLOs)
- Most diversified CLO fund in the market (Eagle Point ~150 positions/60 managers, Fair Oaks ~200/70)
- Manager count is a deal flow visibility advantage from Bain's $58B platform position

## Atom 5: The ABS Bucket

ABS is visible in the portfolio allocation chart but never mentioned in the text. The team has CMBS (Fox), RMBS (Madden), and digital infrastructure (Haswell) specialists. Questions for Nate: What's in the ABS sleeve? Consumer ABS? Equipment? Esoteric? Tactical or structural allocation? How do Fox's CMBS capabilities actually get deployed in SCF?

## Atom 6: Multi-Sector Repositioning Opportunity

The mandate allows rotation across CLO equity, CLO debt (AAA through B), ABS, RMBS, CMBS, and esoteric ABS. Most peers are locked into CLO equity and junior debt only. When CLO arb compresses, SCF can rotate into other sectors. This tactical allocation advantage is invisible in current materials and is the single biggest differentiator vs pure-play CLO funds.

## Atom 7: Fee Structure

- 75bps management fee + 20% carry over 5% hard hurdle
- 0.20% cap on operating expenses
- SMA minimum: $100M with TBD fees (negotiable for large allocators)

Peer comparison:
- Eagle Point ECC: 1.5% mgmt + 20% incentive
- Fair Oaks: ~1.0% + 15%
- Multi-sector structured credit HFs: typically 1.5/20 or 1.25/15
- VanEck CLO Opp (new interval fund): 0.75% (no incentive)
- Cliffwater CCLFX: 0.75% (no incentive)

SCF charges institutional fees but delivers hedge-fund-caliber returns. The 5% hard hurdle is genuinely investor-friendly — Bain doesn't collect carry until the investor clears 5% net. Most "20/5" structures use soft hurdles or catch-ups that functionally pay carry from dollar one. This fee story should be on page 1 of every pitch. Currently buried.

## Atom 8: Liquidity Terms

- Quarterly redemptions, 90 days notice
- 12.5% fund-level gate
- 1-year lockup
- Generous for a CLO equity fund (most run 1-2 year lockups with semi-annual or annual redemptions)
- Eagle Point's public CEFs trade at persistent 10-15% discounts to NAV
- Key question for Nate: Has the fund ever gated? Surviving March 2020 and Q4 2022 without gating would be a powerful talking point.
- SMA structure is critical for $100M+ institutional money wanting custom liquidity terms

## Atom 9: Reinvestment Period Profile

The "Years of Reinvestment Remaining" chart is one of the most important risk metrics and gets zero commentary. CLO reinvestment period = window when the manager can actively trade. Post-reinvestment = amortizing, equity optionality evaporates.

Portfolio concentrated in 1-4 year range with meaningful chunk at 4+ years (healthy). The "<0 years" bucket (post-reinvestment CLOs running off) needs explanation. If Bain controls those positions, they can initiate resets. If minority, they're dependent on whoever controls the deal.

Key question: What percentage of CLO equity positions does Bain control vs minority? Control positions allow directing resets, refis, and calls — a portfolio management lever most allocators underestimate.

## Atom 10: Industry Exposure

Look-through exposure shows healthcare/pharma, high tech, and business services as largest sectors. Broadly consistent with leveraged loan market composition (no massive sector bets). The ability to answer look-through questions ("what's your office CRE exposure?") at the individual loan level is a platform advantage over shops that can only name their CLO managers.

## Atom 11: Composite Growth Gap

GIPS table: composite grew from $150M (2013) to $635M (2017), shrank to $479M (2018, likely redemptions after flat year), bounced around $500-680M through 2024. Current headline: $4.0B "CLO Debt & Equity AUM."

# 3. COMPETITIVE LANDSCAPE & DISTRIBUTION WHITE SPACE

## Direct Competitors

CLO-focused: Eagle Point (ECC/EIC), Fair Oaks, Panagram, Flat Rock, VanEck CLO Opp (new interval fund, Dec 2025), XAI Octagon (XFLT)

Multi-sector: PIMCO, DoubleLine, TPG Angelo Gordon, Ellington, TCW, Marathon, Canyon

Bain's competitive edges:
- Only fund that is BOTH a CLO manager AND a CLO investor
- 75bps + 20/5 hard hurdle = 40-50% cheaper than most alternatives
- 236 positions across 83 managers = most diversified CLO portfolio in market
- Multi-sector mandate allows tactical rotation peers can't match

## White Space Atom 1: Insurance Megatrend (Bigger Than Lincoln)

U.S. insurers grew structured credit from $884B (2014) to $1.44T (2024). ABS/Other Structured doubled to $700B. Insurers hold ~25% of all outstanding U.S. BSL CLOs (~$245B at YE 2024). PE-owned insurers (Athene, Global Atlantic, Everlake) hold 40% of financial/ABS private placements despite controlling just 14% of industry general account assets.

Top 10 active fixed income funds increased structured product allocations from 39.3% (2019) to 47.9% (2025) = $55B+ reallocation away from traditional fixed income.

NAIC RBC Revision Catalyst: NAIC finalizing revised RBC factors for CLOs/ABS expected late 2025/2026. When finalized, every insurance CIO must re-underwrite their structured credit book = consulting-sale-turned-allocation moment. Bain's multi-sector mandate positions SCF to help insurance GAs optimize across full spectrum based on final RBC factors vs competitors offering only CLO tranches.

APAC Insurance Wave: Japan, Taiwan, South Korea, Singapore, Hong Kong implementing new RBC frameworks 2025-2026. First time CLO/structured credit allocations will be capital-efficient for APAC insurers. PineBridge estimates multi-billion-dollar gap: APAC insurers currently 1-2% structured credit vs 15-17% for U.S./Bermuda peers. Japanese banks already major AAA CLO buyers; Japanese insurers logical next step down capital structure.

## White Space Atom 2: CLO ETF Explosion Creates a Funnel

CLO ETFs crossed $35B AUM (Feb 2026), doubled in just over a year. JAAA alone $27B with $2.5B inflows in first 6 weeks of 2026. Janus Henderson now $38B across 15 active securitized ETFs, just launched AA-A CLO ETF. Fidelity entered with two new CLO ETFs, waiving fees year one. Investment-grade CLO ETFs posted 5.7-6.3% total returns in 2025, mezzanine ETFs >7%.

The CLO ETF wave is creating millions of first-time CLO investors who understand the acronym. This is top of funnel. ETF buyers own AAA tranches yielding 4.5-5.5%. Smart investors/advisors will eventually ask: "How do I get the 8-9% version?" Answer: mezzanine debt and equity = SCF's sweet spot. Nobody building the bridge between CLO ETF buyer and institutional CLO equity fund. Educational content gap = Bain opportunity.

## White Space Atom 3: Interval Fund Land Grab

Interval fund assets hit $98B YE 2024, up 31% from 2023. Cliffwater direct lending interval fund alone $32B (from $50M in 2019). 80% of RIAs used interval funds in 2025 (up from 58% in 2024). 58% of RIAs plan to increase private credit allocations in 2026. 70% of RIAs currently allocate to private credit, up from 62% in 2024.

New interval fund filings (late 2025/early 2026):
- VanEck CLO Opportunities Fund (Dec 2025, 0.75% fee, CLO-focused) — DIRECT COMPETITOR at same fee as SCF
- AMG BBH Asset-Backed Credit Fund (Dec 2025, multi-sector structured credit)
- Oaktree Asset-Backed Credit (Jan 2026, wealth platforms)
- Neuberger Berman NB Asset-Based Credit (Aug 2025, specialty finance)
- Blackstone multi-asset credit interval fund (RIA channel specific)

Cliffwater Playbook: Won by focusing exclusively on RIAs, no-load institutional share class, dedicated wholesaler team pitching "access" not alpha. Bain's SCF has better track record than most new entrants, but track record lives inside quarterly-liquidity institutional fund. Arbitrage = wrapping same investment engine in structures wealth channel wants to buy.

## White Space Atom 4: Family Offices Are the Overlooked Channel

Family offices now allocate 42% to alternatives (BlackRock 2025 Global Family Office Report). Private credit most popular: 51% bullish, 32% plan to increase allocations 2025-26 (highest of any alternative). Goldman survey: family office private credit allocation 3% (2023) to 4% (2025), 26% planning further increases. 524% increase in family offices allocating to private markets since 2016.

Gap: Family offices flooding into direct lending but almost none accessing structured credit strategies specifically. SCF's return profile (8.6% net, quarterly liquidity, shallow drawdowns) maps to what family offices want: "equity-like returns with fixed income risk characteristics." Pitch isn't "CLO fund" — it's "absolute return credit strategy with 12-year track record and 40%+ cumulative outperformance."

Endowment/Foundation overlap: 1,150 E&Fs with $100-250M in assets allocating just 20% to alternatives and want more (Cerulli). Exactly right size for commingled fund. E&Fs had weighted average 35.4% alternative allocation in 2024 (Preqin).

## White Space Atom 5: DC Channel (18-24 Month Horizon)

Trump executive order (Aug 2025) opening 401(k) plans to alternative investments. SEC Commissioner Uyeda speech (Nov 2025) calling exclusion of private assets from DC plans "imprudent" and "intellectually dishonest." BlackRock launching target-date fund with 5-20% private market allocation (partnership with Great Gray Trust), debuting H1 2026. DCALTA white paper: 20% allocation to private credit within core bond fund could raise yield from 4.45% to 5.15%. Target-date funds hold nearly $4T.

DC channel won't buy CLO equity funds directly, but multi-manager target-date structures will need "structured credit" allocations as diversifier within private credit sleeves. Bain's track record, institutional pedigree, multi-sector mandate make it natural building block. Firms building these products (BlackRock, State Street, Great Gray, Vanguard/Blackstone) will need sub-advisors with deep structured credit expertise. 2027-2028 revenue stream Bain should be positioning for now.

## White Space Atom 6: Sovereign Wealth Funds

SWFs total $13-14T globally. Over half invest in private credit and plan to increase allocations (Invesco 2024/2025). SWFs allocate ~28% to fixed income. Most have almost zero dedicated structured credit. ADIA, GIC, Temasek, PIF, CIC all actively diversifying fixed income allocations. Bain's $58B platform, multi-sector mandate, SMA flexibility at $100M+ make it credible for sovereign conversations pure-play CLO shops can't access.

## White Space Atom 7: Fee Competitive Advantage (Dramatically Undermarketed)

## White Space Atom 8: Content Gap = Distribution Opportunity

Almost no structured credit educational content aimed at RIAs/wealth advisors. Nobody producing: monthly CLO market one-pagers, interactive waterfall explainers, look-through analytics dashboards, quarterly PM video updates, webinar series, co-branded insurance research. Firms winning interval fund race (Cliffwater, CION) won by educating first, selling second. Bain's platform knowledge (being both CLO manager AND investor) gives unique authority to be educator.

## Five Distribution Plays (Synthesis)

1. Insurance GA Pipeline Beyond Lincoln — Position SCF as capital-charge-optimized multi-sector structured credit solution for NAIC RBC revision cycle. Target PE-owned insurers AND traditional GAs. Expand to APAC insurers.

2. Wealth Channel Acceleration — Interval fund/evergreen wrapper is table stakes. Real play is CLO ETF-to-SCF funnel. Partner with RIA aggregators (Dynasty, Hightower, Focus Financial).

3. Family Office & Mid-Market E&F Direct — Position as "absolute return credit" not "CLO fund." Target 1,150 E&Fs at $100-250M. SMA at $100M captures large family offices.

4. Sovereign and Global Institutional — Bain's 10-office global footprint underutilized. 12-year track record through multiple cycles + multi-sector mandate = what sovereign risk committees seek.

5. DC Channel Positioning (18-24 Month Horizon) — Build relationships with target-date builders now. Being sub-advisor in $100B TDF complex worth more than 100 family office relationships.
