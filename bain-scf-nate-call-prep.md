# Nate Fox Call Prep
## Pre-Meeting Brief for Brett

---

## Call Objective

Get the answers that determine whether the 1-year plan is built on solid ground or needs adjustment. Every question below exists because the answer changes how you'd position SCF, prioritize channels, or sequence the distribution build.

Don't ask all of these. Pick the 8-10 that feel most natural for the conversation. The rest can come up organically in follow-up calls or during the first 30 days.

## THE CRITICAL FIVE (Must-Ask)

### 1. What's the real fund size breakdown?

The one-pager says $4.0B in "CLO Debt & Equity AUM." The GIPS composite table shows $660M at year-end 2024. What's the gap?

Possible answers and what they mean:
- If commingled is ~$700M and the rest is SMAs: Most growth came from large institutional mandates, not the commingled fund. The commingled may be under-marketed or perceived as a feeder to SMA relationships. Distribution strategy should lean heavily on SMA pipeline with the commingled as proof-of-concept.
- If a large capital raise happened in 2025: Need to know the source (consultant-driven? insurance? platform?). That channel is the one already working.
- If $4B includes broader platform AUM: The one-pager is misleading. Fixing the AUM presentation is a quick win.

Follow-up: How many SMAs are currently running? What's the average SMA size? Which investor types are in SMAs vs commingled?

### 2. Who is the current investor base?

Need to understand the composition before chasing new money.

Ask for: LP list by type (insurance, pension, endowment, family office, fund of funds, consultant-driven), approximate check sizes, vintage, and any known re-up timelines.

What this unlocks:
- If heavily insurance: validates the insurance GA channel as primary and shows there's a repeatable playbook
- If mostly fund-of-funds or consultant-driven: consultant penetration matters more than direct origination
- If concentrated in a few large checks: the fund's growth story is lumpy and dependent on whale-hunting, which changes the pipeline approach
- Cross-sell map: Which LPs are also in other BCC products (loan fund, BCSF, direct lending)?

### 3. What has distribution looked like historically, and what hasn't worked?

This is the "where are the landmines" question. You want to know:

- Has there been a dedicated product specialist or distribution person on SCF before? If yes, why did it end?
- What outreach has been done to date? Conference appearances, consultant meetings, RIA channel conversations?
- What objections come up most from allocators?
- Are there channels that were tried and abandoned? (Wealth platforms? Consultants that passed? Insurance GAs that said no?)
- Is there a CRM with prospect history, or is Brett starting from zero?

What this unlocks: Avoids repeating mistakes. If consultants already passed, need to know why before resubmitting. If insurance GAs were approached and declined, need different positioning, not more calls.

### 4. What's the Lincoln partnership status and timeline?

Garver is the Lincoln relationship lead, but Nate will know the strategic context.

Specific questions:
- Is the Lincoln evergreen product live, in development, or still conceptual?
- How does it relate to the commingled fund? Same portfolio, different wrapper? Separate allocation?
- What's Lincoln's distribution model for this? Their advisor network? Institutional GA clients?
- Does the Lincoln partnership create any exclusivity constraints that would limit a separate interval fund or RIA channel strategy?

What this unlocks: If Lincoln is already building the wealth channel wrapper, Brett may not need to propose a separate interval fund. If Lincoln is moving slowly or focused only on their own GA, the door is open for an independent interval fund proposal.

### 5. How does the team think about the multi-sector mandate?

The one-pager reads like a CLO fund, but the portfolio has ABS, and the team has CMBS and RMBS specialists. Need to understand how they actually think about the mandate.

Ask:
- What's in the ABS sleeve? Consumer ABS? Equipment? Esoteric? How big is it relative to the CLO allocation?
- Is ABS tactical (opportunistic when CLO arb compresses) or structural (always have 10-15% in non-CLO)?
- How do Fox's CMBS capabilities and Madden's RMBS background actually get deployed in SCF? Have they been?
- If Bain's $58B platform sources a deal in digital infrastructure ABS or aircraft ABS, does it flow to SCF? Is there a formal deal allocation process?

What this unlocks: If the multi-sector mandate is real and active, the fund should be repositioned from "CLO fund with some ABS" to "multi-sector structured credit." That expands the addressable allocator universe dramatically. If the ABS sleeve is tiny and tactical, keep the CLO positioning but add the optionality as a talking point.

## THE STRATEGIC SIX (Important, Flexible Timing)

### 6. Has the fund ever gated redemptions?

The 12.5% fund-level gate is in the docs. Survived March 2020 and Q4 2022 without triggering it would be a powerful talking point. If it did gate, need to know when, for how long, and how it was communicated to investors.

Why it matters: Insurance GAs and family offices will ask. Having a specific answer ("we experienced X% redemption requests in Q1 2020 and met them in full without gating") is the kind of concrete data point that moves an allocator off the fence.

### 7. Control vs. minority CLO equity — what's the split?

The reinvestment period chart shows both "Control" and "Minority" positions. What percentage of CLO equity positions does Bain control?

Why it matters: Control positions allow Bain to direct resets, refinancings, and calls. That's a portfolio management lever that most allocators don't understand and that pure minority investors can't access. If Bain controls 60-70%+ of their equity book, it's a differentiated talking point about active management at the deal level, not just manager selection.

Follow-up: How many resets/refis did the team execute in 2024 and 2025? What was the average extension of reinvestment period?

### 8. What's the team's bandwidth for distribution support?

PMs get pulled into investor meetings. How much capacity does the current team have?

Ask:
- How many investor meetings does the team currently do per month?
- Who typically leads allocator calls? Nate? Garver? Fox?
- Is there appetite for more external activity (conferences, webinars, content)?
- What's the current cadence for investor communications (quarterly letters, ad-hoc updates)?

What this unlocks: If the team is maxed out, Brett's job becomes shield as much as spear. He'd need to handle early-stage conversations independently and only bring PMs into advanced-stage diligence. If the team wants more visibility, there's appetite for the content engine (quarterly commentary, video updates, webinars).

### 9. What's the team's view on the current market environment?

Not for Brett's education — for positioning. Need to know how the PMs talk about:

- CLO equity: Where are we in the cycle? Is arb healthy or compressed? What happens when base rates normalize?
- CLO debt: Where's the relative value across the capital structure (AAA vs mezzanine)?
- Non-CLO: What's most interesting in ABS/RMBS/CMBS right now? Where are the dislocations?
- Risk: What keeps them up at night? Loan default cycle? Spread compression? Rate trajectory?

What this unlocks: Brett will be repeating the team's market view in every allocator conversation. Need to internalize it accurately, including the nuances and hedges, not just the bullish case.

### 10. Which consultants have SCF in their database?

Some may already be approved. Some may have passed. Some may never have been approached.

Ask:
- Mercer, Cambridge Associates, Aksia, Cliffwater, NEPC, Aon, Wilshire, Meketa — what's the status with each?
- For any that passed: why? What was the feedback?
- For any that approved: how has it translated to pipeline? Are consultants actively recommending SCF?
- Any consultant relationships through other BCC products that could be leveraged for SCF?

What this unlocks: If Aksia already has SCF approved and is recommending it, that's a live pipeline Brett should be activating immediately. If NEPC passed two years ago because AUM was too low, a $4B headline might reopen the conversation.

### 11. Is there appetite for new product structures?

Don't pitch the interval fund yet. But gauge the temperature.

Ask indirectly:
- "Are you seeing demand from allocators for different access points beyond the commingled fund and SMAs?"
- "The Lincoln partnership seems to address part of the wealth channel. Is there interest in doing something more broadly?"
- "Are you tracking what VanEck, Oaktree, and BBH are doing with interval fund filings in structured credit?"

What this unlocks: If Nate says "we've been talking about that" — there's internal momentum Brett can build on. If he says "we're not interested in retail" — Brett knows the interval fund proposal needs to be framed as institutional-quality product in a different wrapper, not as a retail play.

## THE RELATIONSHIP QUESTIONS (Read the Room)

These are less about information and more about understanding how to work effectively with Nate and the team.

### 12. What does the ideal product specialist do for this team?

Listen carefully. Is Nate looking for:
- A marketing person who makes pretty decks? (Positioning matters more than aesthetics)
- A salesperson who generates meetings? (Pipeline activity is the metric)
- A strategic partner who helps shape the product offering? (Broader mandate, including product development)
- Someone who handles the stuff PMs don't want to do? (DDQs, RFPs, consultant databases, operational due diligence)

The answer tells Brett how to calibrate the first 90 days. If Nate wants meetings, front-load outreach. If Nate wants strategic thinking, lead with the market intelligence and let pipeline come from positioning.

### 13. How do decisions get made about fund positioning and materials?

Who approves changes to the one-pager? Is it Nate, Hawkins, compliance, all three? How long does it take?

This determines whether the quick-win supplemental materials approach (from the 1-year plan) is the right move, or whether Brett can actually get a full materials refresh through compliance in the first 60 days.

### 14. What's the relationship between SCF and other BCC credit products?

Are there internal referrals? Competition for allocator dollars? Shared relationships?

If a BCSF investor asks about structured credit, does that get referred to the SCF team? If a loan fund LP wants to diversify, is SCF positioned as the complement? Understanding the internal ecosystem tells Brett where the warm intros live.

## QUESTIONS BRETT SHOULD BE READY TO ANSWER

Nate will likely flip the script. Be prepared for:

"What's your view on the CLO market right now?"
- Have a concise answer. Reference current arb levels, loan default expectations, reinvestment period dynamics. Don't pretend to be a CLO trader. Position as an informed allocator who understands the product.

"Why do you want this role?"
- The honest answer: structured credit is where Brett's career experience converges (CMBS B-piece, commercial real estate finance, structured product analytics) with the biggest distribution opportunity in fixed income. The fund's returns are exceptional and the market is moving toward exactly what SCF offers.

"What would you do in the first 90 days?"
- Have the short version ready: 30 days listening and building materials, 30 days opening insurance GA pipeline (NAIC RBC revision as the hook), 30 days converting and reporting back. Don't promise AUM in 90 days. Promise activity, market intelligence, and a data-informed 12-month roadmap.

"How would you position the fund differently?"
- Lead with: "Multi-sector structured credit, not CLO fund." The mandate flexibility is the differentiator. The fee story (75bps + 20/5 hard hurdle) should be on page 1. The drawdown profile (-8.6% max in 12 years) needs to be a headline, not buried in a table.

"Tell me about your experience with insurance investors."
- Brett's CMBS B-piece background means he's sat across the table from insurance portfolio managers evaluating structured credit. He knows what capital charges mean, how GAs think about spread vs duration vs credit risk, and why the NAIC RBC revision changes the math. Most product specialists learn this from a PowerPoint. Brett learned it from doing the work.
