## Reinvestment Period Explainer (For Allocators Who Ask)
"The reinvestment period is the window during which the CLO manager can actively trade the underlying loan portfolio. It typically runs 4-5 years from deal closing. During this window, the manager can substitute performing loans for defaulted ones, capture trading gains, and maintain portfolio quality. After reinvestment ends, the deal amortizes — cash flows go to pay down debt rather than being reinvested. The equity optionality shrinks.

Our reinvestment chart shows most positions concentrated in the 1-4 year range, which is healthy. The '<0 years' bucket represents post-reinvestment CLOs that are running off. For minority positions, we depend on whoever controls the deal to initiate, but we maintain strong relationships with co-investors and managers."

## THE FOUR COUNTER-ARGUMENTS TO "COMPRESSED ARB" (Upgraded)

NotebookLM strengthened the arb compression rebuttal with additional data. Use all four in sequence:

**Counter 1: The $375-422B Reset Tailwind**
AAA CLO liability spreads at ~120-135 bps over SOFR = historic tights. Combined with $375-422B in non-call expirations, control equity holders can refinance liabilities cheaper and extend reinvestment periods. This is manufactured alpha that minority investors can't access.

Record 2025 numbers: $337B in US CLO refi/reset volume, up from $308B in 2024. The wave is accelerating, not peaking.

**Counter 2: The Quasi-Fixed-Rate Defense**
Both loan assets and CLO debt liabilities float on SOFR. When rates drop, loan yields fall AND debt costs fall in parallel. The spread differential — which drives equity distributions — stays relatively stable. CLO equity is naturally hedged against base rate moves. This is the single most misunderstood feature of CLO equity by non-specialist allocators.

**Counter 3: Long-Term Locked Financing (No Margin Calls)**
CLOs use 12-year locked-in financing with no mark-to-market triggers, no margin calls, no forced sales during volatility. With a long-term loan prepayment average of ~31%, CLO managers get a continuous stream of capital to reinvest par dollars into discounted loans when other market participants are forced to retreat.

**Counter 4: Multi-Sector Escape Valve**
If CLO equity economics become truly unworkable, SCF rotates into CLO debt, ABS, RMBS, CMBS, and esoteric assets where relative value is stronger. Pure-play CLO funds don't have this option. They're trapped. Bain isn't.
